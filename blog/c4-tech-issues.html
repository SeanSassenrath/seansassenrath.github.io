REPRESENTATION OF WOMEN IN THE TECH SPACE

Although I'm not currently in the tech industry, I've found through conversations with friends and associates that the number of women in the tech industry is a glaring problem. According to a report by Yahoo and Google in 2014, only 15% of the Yahoo tech workforce and 17% of the Google tech workforce was female. It's almost an unbelievable statistic considering that women in tech was trending upwards in the 1980's. But since, it has been on somewhat of a nose dive downwards as the chart below shows.

So, what happend? Why the change?

POISONOUS CULTURE

Ironically, one of the most liberal and progressive areas in the world (Silicon Valley) still has a long way to go in creating a non-discriminatory enviornment. The frat-style atmosphere that exists among start-ups and new tech companies have a reputation of objectifing women. This reputation causes women not to persue a career in the tech industry and thus the downward trend.

SOLUTION

I think this issue has to be solved in two ways.

First, there needs to be severe consquences at these companies for those who exibit instances of sexism. It needs to be clearly defined and acknowledged by the entire workforce.

Second, there needs to be an increase in the number of organizations and associations that help bring women into the tech space.
